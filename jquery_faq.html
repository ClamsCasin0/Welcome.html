Channel Islands National Park, California

  • Five of the eight Channel Islands and their surrounding waters are managed by the National Park Service,
  • Sometimes referred to as the "North American Galapagos",
  • there are 145 animals and plants totally unique to this area,
  • These islands are also home to the largest breeding colonies of seabirds in the region,
  • as well as the oldest dated human remains on the continent

Big Bend National Park, Texas

  • They say everything in Texas is larger than average, and this place is no exception.
  • Where the Rio Grande River makes its wide turn through the Chihuahuan Desert,
  • This is a world-class geological area, with laccoliths, faults, volcanoes and remarkable fossils
  • a world-record pterosaur and 50-foot crocodile.
  • Ten-thousand-year-old archeological sites reveal a unique cultural history, and a diversity of wildlife make these wild lands a birder's paradise.

Mammoth Cave National Park, Kentucky

  • Mammoth Cave National Park is by far the world's longest cave system, with 400 miles mapped underground.
  • Well deserving of its name, Treasures inside include gigantic cave formations ("niagara falls" pictured above), as well as 300-million-year-old fossils a
  • visitors can explore 70 forested miles above ground by foot, bike or horse.
  • The Green River winds through these lands as well, offering additional opportunities for boating, fishing and swimming.
  • perfectly preserved artifacts from natives who gathered minerals here 2-4,000 years ago.
